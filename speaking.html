Working with Organisations

Speaking

Keynotes, panels and internal sessions on neurodiversity, inclusion and building workplaces where disabled and neurodivergent people can actually do their best work. I've spoken at the House of Lords, the Cabinet Office, IBM Think, the British Psychological Society, academic and specialist conferences, and internal events for corporates and charities.

Topics I speak on

Neurodiversity at work

What neurodiversity actually means, why representation statistics miss the point, and what organisations need to change — practically, not performatively.

Building genuinely inclusive cultures

The gap between DEI intentions and lived experience. What structural inclusion actually requires — and why getting it right matters beyond optics.

Disability confidence and the business case

Beyond compliance: why disability confidence benefits everyone, and how to move from awareness to action at every level of an organisation.

The neurodivergent professional experience

First-person insight into navigating work as a neurodivergent person — masking, burnout, adjustments, and what genuinely supportive management looks like.

Inclusive hiring and recruitment

Why standard recruitment processes screen out the people you're trying to hire, and what a neurodiversity-informed hiring process actually looks like.

Bespoke topics

I tailor talks for specific audiences — AGMs, leadership away-days, employee resource groups, external conferences and broadcast media. Get in touch to discuss.
