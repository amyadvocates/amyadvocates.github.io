Nervous System Mapping

A tool for noticing what supports, strains, or overwhelms my nervous system.

This is not a test, and it is not fixed forever. It is a way of noticing what feels regulating, what feels manageable but costly, and what feels unsustainable right now. You can use it for environments, work, relationships, routines, tasks, or ways of living.

Regulating

What helps me feel more regulated, resourced, or like myself?

Consider
  • What environments feel calming, spacious, or supportive?
  • What activities help me feel present, energised, or in flow?
  • What people, structures, or routines help me feel safe enough to function well?
  • When do I feel most "like me"?
Lenses sensory · social · work & tasks · environment · daily rhythm
    e.g. quiet mornings · deep-focus time alone · clear written briefs · one close friend at a time

    Costly

    What can I tolerate, but only with limits, support, or recovery?

    0
    Consider
    • What is possible, but draining?
    • What can work short term, but not too often?
    • What tends to tip from okay into too much if I'm tired, stressed, or doing too much elsewhere?
    • What do I need in order to make this manageable?
      e.g. short meetings with clear agendas · a busy café with headphones · one social event per week with recovery time

      Depleting

      What tends to overwhelm, shut me down, or cost too much over time?

      0
      Consider
      • What environments or demands consistently dysregulate me?
      • What do I keep trying to push through, even though it has a high cost?
      • What leaves me depleted, trapped, or like I'm abandoning myself?
      • What might count as an access need or boundary, rather than something I should just "cope with"?
        e.g. open-plan office under fluorescent light · unannounced schedule changes · being called on without warning
